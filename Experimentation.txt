    Reseach Objectives
        To systematically investigate how classical dimensionality reduction influences the expressivity, 
        stability, and alignment performance of quantum kernel methods across different data regimes.
        
            1. Quantify Compression–Expressivity Trade-off
                Identify whether an optimal reduced dimension d′∗ exists.

            2. Analyze the Impact of DR Geometry on Quantum Kernels
                Understand how different geometric assumptions influence kernel structure and alignment.
            
            3. Study Kernel Concentration and Spectral Properties
                Determine whether dimensionality reduction mitigates kernel concentration (barren behavior).

            4. Examine Kernel Alignment Behavior Under Dimensional Constraints
                Determine whether reduced dimensionality leads to more stable and efficient alignment optimization.

        Expected Outcomes (What These Objectives Lead To)
            1. Identification of an optimal dimensionality regime for quantum kernels 
            2. Evidence that dimensionality reduction acts as an implicit regularizer
            3. Insights into how classical preprocessing shapes quantum feature spaces
            4. Practical guidelines for scalable quantum kernel design
